Recommendation Letter Request Instructions

Before requesting a recommendation letter, please ensure you have the following information ready:

  • Your current resume or CV
  • Unofficial transcript
  • List of courses taken with me, including grades
  • Brief statement of your career goals or academic plans
  • Details about the program, job, award, or scholarship you're applying for
  • Specific achievements or projects you want highlighted
  • Deadline for letter submission
  • Any forms or specific instructions for submission
  • Your current contact information
  • A brief explanation of why you chose me for the recommendation
  • Any challenges you've overcome that you'd like mentioned (optional)

Extracurricular activities and leadership roles:

  • SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management) activities
  • Volunteering activities at CSUB
  • Any other HRM-related extracurricular involvements
  • Detailed description of clubs or organizations you've been involved in
  • Specific examples demonstrating leadership, teamwork, or community service
  • Duration and level of involvement in each activity
  • Any awards or recognition received for your extracurricular participation

Undergraduate research experience, including:

  • Research projects you've participated in
  • Names of faculty supervisors
  • Brief description of your role and contributions
  • Any presentations or publications resulting from the research
  • Skills developed through the research experience

How to Request

  1. Compile all the information listed above.
  2. Send an email to spak2@csub.edu with the subject line "Recommendation Letter Request - [Your Name]".
  3. In the email body, briefly state your request and list the programs/positions you're applying to.
  4. Attach your compiled information as a single Word document.

By following these instructions, you'll help me write a strong, personalized recommendation letter that best supports your application.

Q&A: Why Get Your SHRM-CP While You're Still in School?

1. Stand Out in Job Searches

  • Shows employers you're serious about HR (they love that!)
  • Makes your resume pop when applying for internships and entry-level positions
  • You'll have something cool to talk about in interviews

2. Look Like a Pro Early On

  • Having a professional certification while still in school? That's impressive!
  • Companies see certified candidates as ready to jump into real work
  • It's like having a head start in the HR world

3. Save Some Money

  • Student discounts on both SHRM membership and exam fees
  • Way cheaper than waiting until after graduation
  • Your wallet will thank you later!

4. Learn Real HR Skills

  • The exam teaches you stuff you'll actually use in your future job
  • Great practice for solving real workplace problems
  • Builds confidence in your HR knowledge

5. Study Smarter, Not Harder

  • Studying for the exam while taking HR classes? Perfect timing!
  • Your coursework and exam prep will complement each other
  • The material will make more sense since you're learning it in class too

Q&A: How to Prepare for the SHRM-CP Exam

Question 1: How can I get a discount on the SHRM-CP exam fee?

Answer: You can register for a SHRM student annual membership for $75 per year. This membership offers a $100 discount on the SHRM-CP exam fee. You can register for membership at the following link: SHRM Student Membership Registration

Question 2: How do I apply for the SHRM-CP exam, and when should I schedule it?

Answer: You can apply for the SHRM-CP exam through this website: SHRM-CP Exam Application. Make sure to allow at least 2 months to prepare for the exam when selecting your test date.
